Data Privacy and Local Storage

Your privacy is protected by design, not by policy. Octave collects no data because it has no mechanism to do so. There are no servers, no analytics pipelines, no tracking pixels, and no third-party SDKs that report back to anyone.

All user data including playlists, liked songs, listening history, play statistics, and search history is stored exclusively in your device's browser localStorage. This data never leaves your device.

  • No account creation or login required
  • No personal identifiable information collected
  • No cookies set by this application
  • Clearing your browser cache permanently deletes all Octave data
  • The Vault export feature exists specifically so you can back up your own data

Background Play and Browser Limitations

Octave relies entirely on native browser APIs. Background audio playback is subject to the media and battery-saving policies of the browser you are using.

Chrome on Android aggressively suspends background audio tabs. This is a browser limitation, not an Octave bug. For reliable, uninterrupted background playback, Brave Browser is the recommended environment. Brave handles background media sessions correctly and does not interrupt audio when the screen is off or the app is backgrounded.

The developer does not guarantee specific behavior on any browser other than Brave, and accepts no responsibility for interrupted playback caused by browser-level restrictions.

API Availability and Uptime

Octave depends on a pool of public Invidious instances operated by independent third parties. These instances can go offline, rate-limit requests, or return incomplete data at any time for any reason.

When one instance fails, Octave automatically cycles to the next available one in its pool. However, if all instances are unavailable or heavily rate-limited, search and playback will be degraded or unavailable.

The developer makes no guarantees about uptime, search accuracy, or media availability. These are inherent limitations of building on decentralized public infrastructure.
